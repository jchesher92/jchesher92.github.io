About Me

Mathematics was my favourite subject at school and I have always been very fond of problem solving. As a student, I learnt how to implement a wide range of analytical techniques on a variety of datasets using languages such as Matlab, Python and SAS. Furthermore, by studying C++, I was able to gain a better understanding of advanced numerical topics and improve upon my ability to tackle complex mathematical problems.

At the start of this year, I began my career as a financial data analyst for the Office for National Statistics. I enjoyed my role greatly, as I was given the freedom to carry out statistical reports of my own design and accompany them with detailed presentations. However, as an aspiring Software Engineer, I have come to realise that my passion for programming should be the guiding force behind my career change. Recently, I have completed a twelve week Software Engineering Immersive course with General Assembly and have found that this has vastly improved my ability of full-stack development. Moreover, the course has provided a solid foundation with which to pursue creative projects.
